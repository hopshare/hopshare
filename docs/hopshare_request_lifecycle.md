# **Hopshare Hop Lifecycle (Draft)**

## Hop Structure and Lifecycle

Hops are the lifeblood of Hopshare. Without a clearly defined and simple process, Hopshare will not work.

Members create Hops in order to ask for help from other Members. Because people typically feel uncomfortable asking others for help, the whole idea of a time bank is to create some structure around the process to make it feel less intimidating. We use the idea of 'credits' in each Member's time bank account to moderate how Hops are made. Each Hop must provide the following information:

* An estimated number of hours needed.
* A description of what is needed as free form text.
* The timeframe for when the hop needs to be fulfilled (anytime, before/around or on a specific date and time).
* Optional details beyond the description that may be relevant.

Once created, a Hop is publicly available within the Organization for any Member to view. Hopshare will attempt to match Members who might be qualified or interested in helping (see below). At this point, the Hop can potentially be in one of six states:

* Open: the Member who needs help has submitted the Hop to the Organization. One or more Members can indicate they are interested in helping. This creates a notification to the Member who raised the Hop request. That user can then choose which Member they want to have fulfil the Hop request.
  * Accepted: The Member requesting the Hop has indicated which Member they would like to fulfil the Hop.
    * Expired: the timeframe for the hop has passed.
    * Completed: the hop has been fulfilled. This is a terminal state.
  * Canceled: the Member who needs help has withdrawn the Hop. This is a terminal state.
  * Expired: the timeframe for the hop has passed and it is no longer relevant. This is a terminal state.
  * Completed: the hop has been fulfilled. This is a terminal state.

  Open ---------> Accepted -----+
   |                            |
   +------------> Canceled <----+ 
   |                            |
   +------------> Completed <---+
   |
   +------------> Expired


## Fulfilling Hops and Member Communication

After a Member accepts a Hop, the two Members need to coordinate on the logistics to get the Hop fulfilled. Privacy is a major concern on Hopshare, and it is important that we do not require Members to share any more information than necessary. We will suggest to Members that they do not share public information in their hops (such as telephone numbers, addresses, or emails). Members will specify in their profile what their preferred method of contact is (phone number to call or text, or email). When a Hop has been Accepted, Hopshare will provide the accepting Member the hop owner's preferred contact information. From this point on, it is up to the two Members to coordinate on the Hop.

Once the Hop has been fulfilled, it is important for one or both Members to record the activity in Hopshare. This ensures there is a record of the Hop being completed so that credits can be properly exchanged. Either Member marks the Hop as Complete and adds a comment (required) and optionally pictures. Completed Hops are publicly available in the Organization so they can be celebrated by other Members with comments. A success goal for each Hopshare Organization is to have a vibrant, full list of Completed Hops that can be viewed to inspire others to help each other.

If a Hop is fulfilled but neither Member updates the status, Hopshare will email both users asking them to update it. If this still has not occurred after a reasonable amount of time (e.g. a week), Hopshare will mark the Hop as Complete with a standard comment but no credits will be transferred. This motivates Members to update their Hops in a timely fashion.
